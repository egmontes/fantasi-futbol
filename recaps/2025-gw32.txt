📋 GW32 OVERVIEW — Template Merchants Unite

GW32 was a league-wide group project where everyone copied the same Bruno captain and still managed wildly different results. One assist and a yellow somehow became the most popular “success,” while the real points came from chaos agents like Thiago bagging braces and defenders moonlighting as strikers. Add in a swarm of wildcards doing absolutely nothing special, and you’ve got a week where mediocrity was common—but Bambrick chose violence.

⸻

🥇 1ST PLACE — David Bambrick — From Mud to Money

ONE top-4 finish all season… and this is what you do with it? David finally emerges from his five-time bottom-feeder lifestyle and drops an 88 like he’s been competent all year. Bruno captain was fine—an assist and a yellow dressed up nicely by the armband—but the real story is elite decision-making: Thiago in for two goals, Guéhi turning into prime Sergio Ramos, and Bowen casually assisting everything in sight. You even had the audacity to leave an 11-point keeper on the bench and still win. Absolute heater from the league’s most chaotic résumé.

⸻

🥈 2ND PLACE — Logan Kluis — Consistently Second Best

Logan continues his season-long identity: always around, rarely the main character. Sixth top-4 finish is nothing to sneeze at, but this one never felt like a win—more like showing up to a fight with solid technique while Bambrick brought a flamethrower. Bruno captain gave you the same polite 8 points everyone else got, while Thiago and Bowen did the heavy lifting again. Haaland rewarded your faith with a clean-sheet cameo and vibes. Strong season, strong week… still not first.

⸻

🥉 3RD PLACE — Luke Civantos — Sneaky Good, As Usual

Luke quietly notched his fourth top-4 finish like a guy who never says a word but is always somehow ahead of you in the standings. Bruno captain? Sure, whatever, we all did it—but Mukiele and O’Reilly both popping up with goals and clean sheets is how you separate from the pack. Thiago chipped in with his usual two-goal nonsense, and suddenly you’re back in the money without making any noise. Efficient, boring, effective—like a Toyota Camry with a gambling addiction.

⸻

4️⃣ 4TH PLACE — Jimmy / Thomas / Andrew — Shared Delusion of Greatness

Three of you tying for fourth with identical captain choices is the most on-brand thing this league has ever produced. Jimmy continues his absurd streak of NEVER finishing bottom-2—honestly more impressive than winning at this point—while Thomas bags a fifth top-4 thanks to actually doing something useful (hello Guéhi transfer masterclass). Andrew just quietly logs another decent week to add to his solid-but-unspectacular season. Bruno’s assist carried all of you just enough, while Thiago and friends did the rest. Enjoy splitting the fantasy equivalent of loose couch change.

⸻

🍺 SHOTGUN DUTY — Rock Bottom Royalty

Michael Legaz and Mitchel Pattullo, congratulations—you’ve earned a sunrise Guinness.

Legaz went full galaxy brain and captained Semenyo, one of the least-picked options in the entire league, and got a clean sheet plus a yellow for his troubles. That’s not bold—that’s performance art. Meanwhile, Pattullo continues his rollercoaster season (seven top-4 finishes but now back in the basement) by assembling a transfer masterclass in failure: Mayenda didn’t play, Sarr did nothing, and even Haaland as captain somehow felt like the least embarrassing part of the lineup.

Legaz, third trip to the danger zone this season. Pattullo, second visit for a guy who should absolutely know better by now. Set your alarms: 7:30am Saturday. No exceptions. Bring regret.
