Gameweek 19 Recap — When 56 Points Wins You the Week

Summary 😴📉🍻

Gameweek 19 will not be remembered for goals, fireworks, or joy. It will be remembered as the week vibes alone carried us. Scores were low, captains mostly disappointed, and clean sheets quietly decided everything. In fact, things were so underwhelming that 56 points won the week, a five-way tie for 4th happened for the first time ever, and several managers are now staring down early-morning Guinnesses wondering what went wrong.

⸻

🥇🧱 Robert Sneaks 1st — Arsenal Clean Sheet Merchant

@⁨Robert Shellow⁩ takes 1st place for his 4th top-4 finish of the season with the lowest winning score of the year: 56 points. Efficiency at its finest.

He made no transfers, but leaned fully into what he loves most: Arsenal defending. His two Gunners combined for 24 points in the demolition of Aston Villa, with Gabriel returning from injury and casually dropping 15 points like he never left. Toss in a Mateta goal, and that was more than enough in a week where most teams barely moved.

Despite the win, Robert somehow remains firmly midtable, which feels very on-brand.

⸻

🥈🧤 Mitch Pattullo Grinds to 2nd — Wildcard, No Chaos

@⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩ finishes 2nd for his league-leading 5th top-4 finish, staying on top of the money earned leaderboard and moving into the top 3 overall.

He hit the wildcard, but this was not a flashy one. Mitch only got one actual goal, and it came — you guessed it — from Gabriel. The rest of the work was done the quiet way: Pickford’s 10 points, defensive contribution points sprinkled everywhere, and just enough stability to survive a dud-heavy week.

Not exciting. Very effective.

⸻

🥉🧠 JCarr Takes 3rd — Galaxy-Brain Captaincy

@⁨JCarr Gamble⁩ grabs 3rd place for his 3rd top-4 finish of the year thanks to a setup that aged beautifully in hindsight.

He rolled out four defenders, and three delivered clean sheets, which was gold dust this week. The real difference-maker, though, was Matheus Nunes, who dropped 11 points for Manchester City and was owned by just four managers.

JCarr also made the bold call to captain Foden, who only scored 3 points — but in a week where Haaland captains got 2, that was technically a win. Sometimes the bar is underground.

⸻

🏅🤝 Absolute Madness — FIVE-Way Tie for 4th

For the first time ever, we had a five-way tie for 4th featuring @⁨Aidan White⁩, @⁨Tommy Harris⁩, @⁨Lucas Rodriguez⁩, @⁨Luke Civantos⁩, and @⁨Tucker Cina⁩.

All five captained Haaland, all five survived the week in slightly different ways, and none of them did anything especially wrong — or especially right. It was a perfect snapshot of how dull this gameweek truly was.

A fitting end to the first half of the season, and a reminder that sometimes FPL is just vibes and spreadsheets.

⸻

🍺💀 Rob & Katz Sink to the Bottom

@⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ and @⁨Richard Katz⁩ finish in the bottom two spots this week and earn themselves an early-morning Guinness.

They were the only two managers to captain Thiago, which is impressive commitment considering he scored… the same 2 points as Haaland, who most people captained anyway. Unfortunately, the gamble didn’t matter much because neither team managed a single defensive clean sheet, which was the real separator in a week this slow.

Both remain buried in the 30s of the league standings, and this did not help.
