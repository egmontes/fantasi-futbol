🏆⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 31 Recap ⚽🏆

📰 Summary — Free Hit Frenzy & Bench Blunders

Gameweek 31 was dominated by Free Hit chaos, with managers flipping entire squads for one-week glory. Some nailed it perfectly, others… benched half their points. Bruno Fernandes was once again unavoidable, while a few unfortunate captains reminded us that minutes ≠ points. With the Cup right around the corner, every decision is starting to feel a little more serious (for some of you, at least).

⸻

🏆🎯 Cup Reminder — One Week to Impress

The Head-to-Head Cup begins in Gameweek 33, and byes will be determined solely by NEXT gameweek’s score.

So yes — Gameweek 32 is your audition.
Some of you are fighting for a bye… others are just fighting to avoid public embarrassment.

⸻

🥇🤝 Mark & Eric — Free Hit Royalty

👑 @⁨Mark Wright⁩ — Top 10 Arrival

Mark grabs a share of 1st place and officially breaks into the top 10. He used his Free Hit to perfection, captaining Bruno Fernandes, who delivered a goal and an assist against Bournemouth.

He also started Gordon (goal), Harry Wilson (goal), and smartly doubled up on Aston Villa defense, which paid off with a clean sheet against West Ham. Add in Rogers’ assist, and Mark’s squad was firing across every line.

⸻

🎯 @⁨Eric Wallerstein⁩ — Quietly Climbing

Eric matches Mark at the top and continues his steady climb toward the top half of the table. Like Mark, he nailed the Free Hit + Bruno captain combo.

Eric’s squad had a slightly different flavor: Gibbs-White and Raúl Jiménez both scored, while Kelleher anchored things with 9 points thanks to Brentford’s clean sheet against Leeds. Add in Gordon’s goal, and it was a well-balanced, high-output lineup.

⸻

🥉🤝 Graham & Andrew — Same Result, Different Journeys

📈 @⁨Graham Derfner⁩ — No Chip, No Problem

Graham continues his strong season with another top finish, now sitting 3rd overall. He didn’t even need a chip to get here.

He captained Bruno Fernandes, had Harry Wilson, and got support from Rogers and Milenković, who added 9 points with a clean sheet for Nottingham Forest. Just a solid, composed week from a manager who knows what he’s doing.

⸻

🔄 @⁨Andrew Denucci⁩ — Free Hit Lifeline

Andrew ties for 3rd, but the context is very different — he’s still hanging around near the bottom of the table.

He used his Free Hit and got strong returns from Gibbs-White, Gordon, Kelleher, and Harry Wilson, alongside the standard Bruno captaincy. A much-needed bounce-back week… now the question is whether he can do it without deleting his entire team again.

⸻

🍺🔥 Bottom Two — A Masterclass in What Not to Do

🧊 @⁨Tyler Wasser⁩ — The 7-Minute Captain

Tyler lands in the bottom two for the 3rd time this year, and honestly, it takes skill to mess up a Free Hit this badly.

He captained Ekitike, who played a grand total of 7 minutes. Seven. Minutes. That’s barely enough time to tie your boots, never mind score points.

Yes, he had Bruno Fernandes like everyone else, but the rest of his team clearly thought they were on a bye week. A full squad overhaul… for that.

⸻

🤯 @⁨Dave Bambrick⁩ — Bench FC Hall of Fame

Dave joins Tyler at the bottom and earns his league-leading 5th Guinness shotgun. And this one… this one is special.

He also used his Free Hit, captained João Pedro (77 minutes, 0 returns — impressive), and got a goal from Harry Wilson. Sounds salvageable, right?

Wrong.

Dave somehow managed to bench BOTH Bruno Fernandes AND Gordon — a casual 23 points sitting there doing absolutely nothing. That’s not bad luck. That’s art. That’s a commitment to chaos that few can replicate.

Dave remains firmly in last place, and at this point, the only consistent thing about his team is the weekly disaster.

⸻

🍻 The Consequences

Tyler and Dave now owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET.

Dave might want to do two just for that bench alone.

⸻

Gameweek 31 wraps up with Free Hit highs and catastrophic lows. One week left before the Cup cutoff…

Choose wisely. Or don’t — the content has been incredible either way 🍻⚽
