📝 Gameweek 25 Summary

Gameweek 25 delivered chaos, late goals, loyalty paying off, accidental geniuses, and some truly cursed teams. Haaland remembered he’s Haaland, Bruno was everywhere (again), Arsenal defenses were basically free money, and two managers are now spiritually preparing for an early-morning Guinness shotgun. Let’s get into it.

⸻

🥇 1st Place: Mike — “Patience Is a Virtue”

@⁨Mike Delaus⁩ takes the top spot for his 3rd top-four finish of the season, and he did it the old-fashioned way: smart transfers and unwavering faith in Erling Haaland. His two free transfers paid off with a net gain of 11 points, highlighted by Bruno Fernandes getting on the scoresheet. Add in two Arsenal clean sheets, goals from Rogers and Bruno Guimarães (who also tossed in an assist), and a long-awaited Thiago goal, and this was a very tidy week.

Mike captained Haaland, who finally woke up with a late goal and assist against Liverpool, rewarding the loyalty. With this performance, Mike is officially knocking on the door of the top half of the table.

⸻

🥈 2nd Place: Eddy — “Calculated Chaos”

Eddy bags 2nd place for his 4th top-four finish by embracing the bold life. He captained Mbeumo, who delivered with a goal in United’s win over Spurs, and doubled down with Bruno Fernandes as well. His strike force of Haaland and João Pedro came through, with João Pedro drawing two penalties that counted as assists.

The real galaxy-brain moment, though, was starting Hill from Bournemouth — a move Eddy only made because Van Hecke didn’t play. Hill rewarded the accidental genius with 7 points from defensive work and an assist. Eddy stays just inside the top 10, quietly cooking.

⸻

🥉 3rd Place: Mitch Pattullo — “Buy-In Secured”

@⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩ lands 3rd place, marking his 6th top-four finish of the season, tying for league best. With this result, he becomes the 3rd manager to officially make his buy-in back, and he rockets five spots into the top three overall.

He captained Bruno Fernandes, doubled up with Mbeumo, and got solid contributions from Bruno Guimarães, Thiago, and Haaland. The masterstroke? Mitch was the only manager to start Dalot, who rewarded him with an assist and a clean sheet.

The only blemish was an unnecessary extra transfer that resulted in a –4 hit, which was just enough to keep him out of first this week — but still an elite performance.

⸻

🏅 4th Place: Robert — “Set It and Forget It”

@⁨Robert Shellow⁩ finishes 4th this week, earning his 6th top-four finish of the season, tied for the league lead. He remains comfortably atop the money-made table and climbs four spots to just inside the top half of the league standings.

Despite having five free transfers (a strong sign his team has been on autopilot), Robert still delivered. He captained Haaland, got goals from Rogers and Mbeumo, and leaned heavily on what he trusts most: Arsenal’s defense. Three Arsenal clean sheets later, and the man is once again cashing checks without lifting a finger.

⸻

🍺 Bottom Two: Michael & Tyler — “Shotgun Secured”

It was a rough one for @⁨Michael Legaz⁩ and @⁨Tyler Wasser⁩, who finish in the bottom two and now owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on Saturday, Feb 14th.

Tyler took a –4 hit that included the tragic decision to sell Haaland right before his haul. While captain João Pedro showed up, the rest of Tyler’s squad combined for zero goals, zero assists, and just one clean sheet.

Michael, meanwhile, still has five free transfers, and it shows. Of his 15-man squad, six players didn’t play a single minute. Even captaining Haaland couldn’t save a week that was doomed before kickoff.
