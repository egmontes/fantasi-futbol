🏆⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 29 Recap ⚽🏆

📰 Summary — João Pedro Nukes the League

Gameweek 29 was defined by one man: João Pedro. While most of the league chased other captains, one brave soul hit the jackpot with a hat trick and assist explosion. Arsenal defenders printed points, clean sheets were everywhere, and a few managers quietly climbed the standings while others continued their long, slow descent toward Guinness shotguns.

⸻

🥇🔥 JCarr — The João Pedro Jackpot

@⁨JCarr Gamble⁩ takes 1st place for his 5th top-four finish of the season and becomes the 4th manager to make his buy-in back. This dominant week rockets him five spots up the table to 4th overall.

He made two transfers for a +9 net gain, led by Saka scoring a goal, but the real story was the captaincy masterstroke. JCarr was the only manager in the league to captain João Pedro, who responded by casually dropping a hat trick and an assist against Aston Villa. Absolute nuclear outcome.

The rest of his squad supported the explosion nicely: two Arsenal defenders combined for 24 points, Bruno Fernandes chipped in an assist plus 3 bonus, and Semenyo kept his hot streak alive with another goal. When your captain does that much damage, the rest of the team just needs to stay out of the way.

⸻

🥈⚡ Liam Kelly — The Transfer Technician

@⁨Liam Kelly⁩ grabs 2nd place for his 4th top-four finish, inching closer to the top half of the table.

He made three transfers for a massive +15 gain, with Bruno Fernandes and Dewsbury-Hall leading the way — the latter scoring a goal. Liam also started João Pedro, though he opted to captain Semenyo instead.

Defensively, things were rock solid: two Arsenal defenders delivered 18 points, and Senesi added another 11 thanks to Bournemouth’s clean sheet against Brentford. Efficient transfers and steady returns across the board powered this strong finish.

⸻

🥉🤝 Zach & Tommy Mayer — Defense Wins Championships

🧱 @⁨Zach Duguid⁩ — The Defensive Fortress

Zach ties for 3rd place, marking his 5th top-four finish of the season. He climbs the standings and edges closer to the top 10.

His captain Virgil unfortunately blanked in Liverpool’s loss to Wolves, but Zach’s defense more than made up for it. His five other defensive players averaged over 10 points each, which is borderline ridiculous production from the back line. Add Bruno Fernandes and Semenyo contributing again, and the points stacked up quickly.

⸻

😴 @⁨Tommy Mayer⁩ — The “No Transfers, No Problem” Strategy

Tommy Mayer also lands 3rd, earning his 4th top-four finish of the year — and he did it without making a transfer in weeks.

He captained Bruno Fernandes, started João Pedro and Semenyo, and watched his two Arsenal defenders combine for 24 points. He was also one of just three managers to start Everton’s Garner, who chipped in 10 points.

The only hiccup was starting Van de Ven, whose red card delivered a painful –3 points. Even so, Tommy’s set-it-and-forget-it approach continues to somehow work.

⸻

🍺💀 Bottom Two — The Goalkeeper Crisis

😬 @⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ — Shotgun King

Ryan finishes in the bottom two again, earning his league-leading 5th Guinness shotgun. At this point he may need a sponsorship.

He hasn’t made a transfer in weeks and rolled the dice by captaining Beto, who unfortunately did absolutely nothing. Outside of his goalkeeper’s clean sheet (in a week where everyone seemed to have one) and Semenyo’s goal, his team completely disappeared.

⸻

🫠 @⁨Gabriel Costa⁩ / @⁨Daniel T⁩annenbaum — No Goalkeepers, No Chance

The co-managers Gabriel and Daniel also land in the bottom two, collecting their 3rd Guinness shotgun of the season. They captained Ekitike like much of the league, but he delivered a dud.

Other than Gabriel’s 11 points (which most of the league had anyway), there wasn’t much to celebrate. The real disaster? Neither their starting goalkeeper nor their backup played a single minute. Hard to win fantasy games without a goalkeeper.

They remain 30th or worse in the league standings and searching for answers.
