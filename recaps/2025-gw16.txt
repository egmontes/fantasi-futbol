Gameweek 16 Recap — Fantasí Fútbol

Summary 🌪️💸⚽

Gameweek 16 was all about following the formula: Haaland goals, Bruno brilliance, Rogers cooking, and Man City defenders quietly printing points. Logan somehow turned all of that into the most money earned in the league while still hanging out below mid-table, John and Mitch kept climbing with near-identical blueprints, and Jack proved that using five transfers and a bench boost can still go horrifically wrong. Also, Jack continues his historic pace toward a Guinness-based Hall of Fame.

⸻

🥇 Logan Kluis Takes 1st — Rich in Cash, Poor in Rank 💰🚀🤯

@⁨Logan Kluis⁩ wins the week and picks up his third top-4 finish, vaulting himself into the most money earned spot with $65, despite still not being in the top half of the table. Efficiency king.

His Man City triple-up was flawless:
	•	Haaland (captain) banged in two goals
	•	His two City defenders combined for 19 points with a clean sheet
	•	His other two defenders also kept clean sheets, because why not

The midfield was even nastier. Semenyo scored, Rogers (a transfer in) exploded with two goals, and Bruno Fernandes added a goal and an assist. Logan basically solved the week like a math problem.

⸻

🥈 John King Goes Back-to-Back — Quietly Dangerous 👑📈🧠

@⁨John King⁩ finishes second for the second week in a row, earning his third top-4 finish and jumping seven spots, now knocking on the door of the top 10.

He followed the script:
	•	Haaland captained
	•	Bruno and Rogers both delivering
	•	Saka with two assists
	•	Foden scoring yet again

Defense was light with just one clean sheet, but John made up for it by being one of only two managers to start Bowen, who found the net again. Clean, calm, and climbing.

⸻

🥉 Mitch Pattullo in 3rd — Trust the Process 🔄📊🔥

@⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩ lands in third place for his third top-4 finish, jumping six spots into the top 10 and announcing himself as a serious threat.

He used all five free transfers for a +19 point swing, and the decisions were spot-on:
	•	Bruno captained (money move)
	•	Haaland, Rogers, and Foden all in place

There was a very clear way to succeed this week, and Mitch executed it like he had the answer key.

⸻

🏅 Eric Wallerstein Takes 4th — Transfer Wizardry 🪄🔵📉➡️📈

@⁨Eric Wallerstein⁩ grabs 4th place and his second top-4 finish, climbing five spots and sitting just outside the top 10.

He went full throttle with five free transfers, resulting in a massive +29 point swing, driven mostly by:
	•	Bringing in Bruno and Rogers
	•	Captaining Saka for his big week
	•	Two Man City defenders combining for 14 points
	•	And yes, Haaland, obviously

A very efficient week that sets Eric up nicely for a continued push.

⸻

🍺 James & Jack Bottom Out — Guinness on Deck 😵‍💫🥴🚨

@⁨James McMullen⁩ and @⁨Jack Bernatchez⁩ finish in the bottom two and must shotgun a Guinness before 7:30am ET tomorrow.
	•	This is Jack’s league-leading 4th shotgun
	•	James picks up his 2nd

James made no transfers, captained Mbuemo (while United scored four goals anyway), and somehow escaped the week with zero defensive clean sheets.

Jack went the opposite direction: five transfers plus a bench boost, which somehow still failed spectacularly. His bench added just 7 points, and the fatal blow was captaining Thiago, who delivered absolutely nothing. Both managers now sit in the bottom four of the standings.
