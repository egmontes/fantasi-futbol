⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 7: Haaland and the Case of the 90-Point Monster

Summary:
Gameweek 7 had everything — record-breaking scores, bold captaincy calls, and another reminder that ignoring Haaland is a form of fantasy self-harm. Robert Shellow detonated the league with a 90-point bomb, James pulled off an all-time rogue move to win the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week Award, and Jimmy’s quietly turning into a problem again. Meanwhile, Ryan Kelly and Richard Katz both earned themselves a frothy 7:30am Guinness for their crimes against team management.

⸻

🥇@⁨Robert Shellow⁩ Sets the League Ablaze

Robert Shellow took no prisoners this week. Without making a single transfer, he exploded for 90 points, the highest score of the season so far. Haaland (captained, obviously) did his thing again, but it was Robert’s supporting cast that truly popped off. Double Arsenal defense locked in 15 points, Grealish came to life with 10, and Kudus added a saucy 12. It’s Robert’s 2nd top 4 finish of the year, vaulting him into the top 5 of the league and tying him for most money earned so far. No transfers. No nonsense. Just pure fantasy domination.

⸻

🥈@⁨Matt Nicolai⁩ Reclaims His Crown Energy

The reigning champ is back. Matt Nicolai, last year’s runaway title winner, reminded everyone who’s boss with his first top 4 finish of the season. He also captained Haaland (duh), and his decision to bring in Gabriel (9 points) proved clutch. Matt was one of only three managers to start Dan Burn, who rewarded him with an 11-point clean sheet and goal combo. Toss in Kudus for dessert, and Matt’s suddenly back in the top half of the table, looking like he never left.

⸻

🥉 @⁨James McMullen⁩ and the Semenyo Supremacy

Every season, there’s that one manager who completely ignores logic and gets rewarded for it. This week, it’s James, who didn’t just start Semenyo—he captained him. Two goals and an assist later, James took home the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week Award and a shiny 3rd-place finish. He also had Kudus cooking and got extra help from low-ownership picks Mbuemo and Woltemade, who combined for 12 points. Oh, and the kicker? James doesn’t even own Haaland. Absolute chaos merchant energy.

⸻

🎯 @⁨Jimmy Romanelli⁩ Finds His Groove

Back-to-back top 4 finishes for Jimmy, who’s quietly climbing the table after a slow start. Like most mortals, he captained Haaland, and like most smart managers, he had Kudus. But Jimmy also flexed his differential muscle—he was one of only three managers to start Timber, who rewarded him with a clean sheet and an assist for Arsenal. Jimmy’s heating up, and if this form continues, the league might need to start worrying about another Jimmy takeover.

⸻

🍺 Guinness Time for @⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ and @⁨Richard Katz⁩

Every league needs a reminder that bad management has consequences—and this week, Ryan Kelly and Richard Katz were those reminders. Both managers land in the bottom two, meaning they owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on either Saturday, October 11th or October 18th (a generous grace period thanks to the international break). It’s Katz’s first bottom finish and Ryan’s league-leading third. Neither made a transfer. Neither owns Haaland. Both captains blanked. To make matters worse, they had enough points rotting on their benches to avoid this fate. Brutal scenes.
