Gameweek 22 Recap — Clean Sheets & Chaos

Summary 🧼🛡️🤡

If you didn’t have clean sheets this week, you simply didn’t have a good time. Defenders and goalies carried the league, captaining attackers was optional (and sometimes pointless), and one accidental sub turned into league-saving brilliance. Meanwhile, the bottom two produced a performance so dry it deserves its own drought warning.

⸻

🥇🧠 Zach Cleans Up (Literally)

@⁨Zach Duguid⁩ takes 1st place for his 4th top-4 finish of the year and officially plants himself in the top half of the table.

This was a defensive masterclass. All four defenders and his goalie held clean sheets, including a double-up on Everton defense, which felt illegal but paid off. Bruno Fernandes chipped in with 10 points against Man City, but the real flex was Zach’s captaincy.

Zach earns the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week 🍺🏆 by captaining Gabriel, turning a clean sheet into pure profit. No Haaland dependency, no nonsense — just clean sheets and confidence.

⸻

🥈🧤 Luke Goes Full Brick Wall

@⁨Luke Civantos⁩ finishes 2nd for his 3rd top-4 finish of the season and jumps into the top 10 like he owns the place.

Despite captain Haaland doing absolutely nothing, Luke still cruised thanks to — you guessed it — all four defenders and his goalie keeping clean sheets. His keeper Sanchez went nuclear with 11 points, courtesy of save after save after save.

Add in Wirtz scoring again during his hot streak, and Luke proves once more that captains are optional when your defense refuses to concede.

⸻

🥉🎯 Ryan Gets Bold (and It Works)

@⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ lands 3rd place for his 2nd top-4 finish of the year and finally gets some breathing room near the bottom of the table.

He grabbed three defensive clean sheets, survived a landmine from O’Reilly’s –1, and locked it all in by boldly captaining Wirtz — a move that paid off beautifully.

Risk was taken. Reward was earned. The climb out of the basement has officially begun 📈

⸻

🏅🍀 Eddy’s Accidental Brilliance

Eddy finishes 4th for his 3rd top-4 finish of the year and inches dangerously close to the top 10 👀

He rolled out Sanchez plus three clean sheets from defense, added Wirtz, and then hit the fantasy equivalent of a scratch-off jackpot. Eddy was the only manager in the league to start Dorgu, who scored a goal AND kept a clean sheet against Man City 🤯

Was it genius? Maybe.
Was it luck? Absolutely — Dorgu only came in because Nunes didn’t play.
Still counts. No notes.

⸻

🍺🚨 Bottom Two Disaster: Gabe/Daniel & Tommy Harris

Pain. Pure pain.

@⁨Gabriel Costa⁩/@⁨Daniel T⁩annenbaum and @⁨Tommy Harris⁩ finish in the bottom two, meaning:
	•	Tommy’s 3rd shotgun of the year
	•	Gabe/Daniel’s 2nd 🍻😬

Tommy’s week was carried entirely by Romero’s 8-point goal, and that was it.
Gabe/Daniel managed Enzo’s lone assist, and… nothing else.

No goals.
No clean sheets.
No joy.

A truly brutal week where fantasy points were rationed like wartime supplies.

⸻

That’s Gameweek 22. Defenders ruled, captains got weird, and the Guinness queue keeps growing. On to the next one 🍺⚽️
