🍻 Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 5 Recap: The Week Nobody Transferred

Apparently, 38 managers in our league collectively decided to treat the transfer button like it’s radioactive, because the entire top 4 this week made zero transfers. Truly inspiring laziness. Let’s dive in:

⸻

🥇 @⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩ — Ekitike Enthusiast

Mitch grabbed his first ever top-4 finish and did it in style. His captain Ekitike delivered a goal, his Man City duo Haaland and Reijnders both chipped in 9, and he was one of seven geniuses to start Neco Williams, who scored from defense.

With no transfers made, Mitch just shrugged, trusted his guys, and walked into first place with 13th overall in the standings. Sometimes doing nothing is the best strategy — or at least it beats doing whatever Ryan Kelly has been doing.

⸻

🥈 @⁨Mike Delaus⁩ — Transfer-Free Triumph

Mike also made zero transfers this week, proving that the “set and forget” strategy can sometimes actually work. He captained Salah (assist merchant this week), had the Reijnders–Neco Williams combo, and got goals from Bruno Fernandes and Mateta to climb into 2nd.

The reward? His first top-4 finish of the season and a jump into the top half of the league at 17th place. Not bad for someone who didn’t even bother to tinker.

⸻

🥉@⁨Tyler Wasser⁩ — The Differential Daredevil

Tyler joined the “no transfer” club and still managed his first top-4 finish. He captained Ekitike and got some help from Kudus’s assist. But what really separated him was a pair of wild differentials:
	•	One of only 2 managers to start Chalobah, who scored in Chelsea’s loss.
	•	One of only 3 managers to start Ndiaye, who delivered 6 points even while Everton were busy losing again.

Turns out, trusting random guys nobody else starts is sometimes… galaxy brain.

⸻

🎯 @⁨Aidan White⁩ — Dubravka the Hero

Aidan pulled his second top-4 finish this year and soared up to 4th overall in the standings. And of course — no transfers. But his week came down to one gutsy call: starting Dubravka over Sanchez.

Dubravka gave him 4 points. Sanchez? Red card, -3. That’s a 7-point swing that made all the difference. From now on, Aidan should probably get “Trust Dubravka” tattooed on his arm.

⸻

🍺 @⁨Tucker Cina⁩ & @⁨Jack Bernatchez⁩ — The Guinness Boys, Volume III

The bottom two this week were Tucker (his first shotgun debt) and Jack (his second, tying the league lead in beers owed). Both avoided transfers like it’s a religion, and both paid the price.
	•	Tucker captained Joao Pedro, who ghosted harder than Chelsea’s title hopes. To add insult, he left Reijnders’ 9 points on the bench.
	•	Jack… oh Jack. His only “meaningful” points came from Semenyo and Caicedo’s defensive stats. No goals. No clean sheets. Just pure sadness. He’s now officially bottom of the entire league. Someone check on him.
