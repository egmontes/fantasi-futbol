🏆⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 30 Recap ⚽🏆

📰 Summary — Clean Sheets Carry & Cup Chaos Looms

Gameweek 30 was all about clean sheets and captaincy hits, with defenses doing the heavy lifting while some big-name captains… simply did not show up (literally). Bench Boosts were flying, Haaland continued his disappearing act, and the bottom of the table delivered absolute comedy. Meanwhile, the Cup is right around the corner, and the pressure is starting to build.

⸻

🏆🎯 Cup Reminder — It’s Coming Fast

The Head-to-Head Cup begins in Gameweek 33, and byes will be determined solely by your Gameweek 32 score.

In other words: the next couple weeks actually matter… even for those of you currently vibing in 34th place.

⸻

🥇💪 Mitch Maisel — No Transfers, No Problem

@⁨Mitch Maisel⁩ takes 1st place for his 4th top-four finish and rockets up to 2nd overall in the league table.

The best part? He made zero transfers. Pure confidence.

He captained Bruno Fernandes, who delivered 2 assists in the win over Aston Villa. His Brentford duo combined for 14 points thanks to Thiago’s goal and Dango’s assist, while his defense absolutely carried: Gabriel, Senesi, and Van Hecke all held clean sheets and added defensive contributions.

Even Raya got in on the action with Arsenal’s clean sheet. It wasn’t flashy — just a complete, well-rounded performance across the board.

⸻

🥈🤝 Tommy Harris & Liam Cashman — Bench Boost Bros

🔄 @⁨Tommy Harris⁩ — The Bournemouth Believer

Tommy Harris grabs a share of 2nd place and stays just inside the top half of the table. He deployed the Bench Boost and leaned heavily into Bournemouth, tripling up on their defense, which paid off with a clean sheet.

Even though his Brentford defenders on the bench didn’t keep a clean sheet, Kayode still delivered 8 points with an assist, which softened the blow. A bold, slightly chaotic strategy that somehow worked.

⸻

📈 Liam Cashman — Minus 4, Plus 15

@⁨Liam Cashman⁩ also takes 2nd, and his week was a rollercoaster. He used an extra transfer for a –4 hit, but somehow turned it into a +15 net gain. That’s elite recovery.

Like Tommy, he used his Bench Boost, with Dubravka’s clean sheet for Burnley adding a crucial 6 points from the bench.

The funniest part? He captained Ekitike, who didn’t even start and did absolutely nothing — and still finished 2nd. That’s the kind of week this was.

⸻

4️⃣⚔️ Rob — Captain Thiago Strikes Again

@⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ secures 4th place for his 6th top-four finish of the season and continues hovering just below mid-table.

He stayed true to his identity and captained Thiago, who rewarded him (again) with a goal. At the back, his two Arsenal defenders combined for 20 points, continuing the league-wide defensive feast.

Rob was also the only manager to start Thiaw, who chipped in 9 points — a nice little differential to round out a strong week. No transfers, no stress, just steady returns.

⸻

🍺🔥 Bottom of the Barrel — Public Execution

🪦 @⁨Logan Kluis⁩ — The Anti-Transfer Pioneer

Logan finishes dead last, which is honestly impressive given how many chances he’s had to… do literally anything. Weeks without transfers, Haaland captain in the middle of a slump, and somehow the confidence never wavered. Bold strategy to just ignore reality and hope it fixes itself. It did not.

⸻
