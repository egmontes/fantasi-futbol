GW34: WILDCARDS & WAR CRIMES

📋 GW34 HEADLINES
🥇 Eric Wallerstein wildcards his way to glory and makes the rest of you look like you picked your team blindfolded
🥈 Tyler Wasser nearly doubles Gibbs-White into orbit but still finds a way to finish second (on brand)
🥉 Rob DiSanto free hits his way onto the podium and immediately reminds us he actually knows ball

🍺 Jack Bernatchez and John Heyer combine for 40 points and a 7:30am Guinness nightmare

⸻

🥇 1ST PLACE — Eric Wallerstein — Wildcard Masterclass

Eric hit the wildcard button and immediately looked like he had access to insider trading. Captained Watkins, who didn’t exactly explode, but the real damage came from Igor Jesus casually dropping a goal, two assists, and a clean sheet like he was playing FIFA on beginner mode. Add in Gibbs-White and Casemiro hauling, and suddenly Eric’s sitting on 78 like it was light work. Fifth top-4 finish on the season and zero bottom-2s — this isn’t luck anymore, it’s a problem for the rest of you.

⸻

🥈 2ND PLACE — Tyler Wasser — Gibbs-White Merchant

Tyler strapped the armband to Gibbs-White and watched him go nuclear — a goal, an assist, and bonus points turning into a massive captain return that carried this entire team. Bowen chipped in with two assists because apparently everyone owns him now, and Gabriel quietly did his clean sheet thing. Three top-4 finishes now, but four bottom-2s lurking in the background like unpaid parking tickets — Tyler’s season is basically a coin flip with emotional damage attached. So close to first, but never quite escaping the chaos.

⸻

🥉 3RD PLACE — Rob DiSanto — Free Hit, Real Hit

Rob rolled out the free hit and, shockingly, used it correctly. Watkins captain was fine, not spectacular, but the supporting cast of Gibbs-White, Casemiro, and Bowen did the heavy lifting like a well-oiled machine. Seven top-4 finishes now — one of the most consistent performers in the league — yet somehow still feels like he’s lurking instead of dominating. This was clinical, efficient, and just annoying enough to remind everyone he’s always around.

⸻

4️⃣ 4TH PLACE — Liam Cashman — Just Enough FC

Cashman captained Bruno Fernandes and got exactly what that deserved — a modest assist doing just enough once doubled to keep him relevant. Casemiro and Raya were the real heroes here, dragging this team into the money while Bruno rode shotgun. Six top-4 finishes on the season now, so the consistency is real, but this felt like sneaking into fourth while everyone else tripped over themselves. Ugly, effective, paid.

⸻

🍺 SHOTGUN DUTY — Dawn Patrol Disasters

Jack Bernatchez and John Heyer, congratulations — you’ve earned yourselves a sunrise Guinness and the shame that comes with it. Jack’s 22 points included captaining Thiago, which is less a strategy and more a cry for help, while John Heyer’s 18-point disasterclass somehow featured a Bruno captain that still couldn’t save him. Jack now racks up another bottom-2 to add to the collection, while Heyer continues his descent into full-blown weekly liability status. Alarm clocks set for 7:30am Saturday — no excuses, no mercy, and someone please film it.
