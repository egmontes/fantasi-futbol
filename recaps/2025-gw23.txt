Gameweek 23 Recap — Bench Boosts, Cold Captains & Chaos

Summary 🎭📉📈

This was a week where smart differentials printed money, benches actually mattered (for once), and captaining Haaland felt like buying stock at the very top. One manager cashed out, several managers cooked… and two managers are now setting early alarms for Guinness-related reasons.

⸻

🥇💸 Logan Breaks the Bank

@⁨Logan Kluis⁩ takes 1st place for his league-tied 5th top-4 finish and becomes only the second manager all season to earn back the full $100 buy-in 💰👏 He also rockets up 7 spots into the top 10.

This was a clinic. Logan fired the bench boost, and it paid off beautifully. Matheus Nunes and Bowen both delivered double-digit hauls, while Fulham’s Anderson chipped in 8 points — a move only three managers even attempted.

The bench itself added 17 points, led by Neco Williams’ 8 points, which Logan was the only manager to benefit from. Absolute sicko behavior.

And the wildest part? Even without the bench boost, Logan still would’ve finished 3rd this week. All of this happened while his captain — Haaland — didn’t even start and continued his icy cold run. Historic week.

⸻

🥈🦁 John King’s Balanced Attack

@⁨John King⁩ finishes 2nd for his 4th top-4 finish of the year, inching closer to the top half of the table.

He joined the Bowen train, captained Haaland like the rest of us, and still found points everywhere else. His newly acquired Man City duo Semenyo and Guehi combined for 14 points, while Aston Villa’s Rogers and Konsa matched that with another 14.

Add in Van de Ven scoring a goal, and John quietly put together one of the most well-rounded teams of the week.

⸻

🥉🎢 Gabriel/Daniel: From Basement to Podium

After finishing dead last last week, @⁨Gabriel Costa⁩/@⁨Daniel T⁩Anne Baum rebound in a big way with 3rd place and their 3rd top-4 finish of the season.

They feasted on Man City’s clean sheet with Matheus Nunes and Guehi, added goals from Van de Ven and Harry Wilson’s late Fulham winner, and even got midfield goals from Rogers and Enzo.

Unfortunately, this otherwise great week will forever be remembered for one thing:
triple-captaining Haaland… for 1 point 😬😬😬

A bounce-back week — emotionally complicated, but effective.

⸻

🏅🔥 Liam Cashman Keeps Rolling

@⁨Liam Cashman⁩ lands 4th place for his 3rd top-4 finish of the year and holds steady just inside the top half of the table.

He rode the Matheus Nunes, Guehi, and Harry Wilson wave, grabbed 5 points from Bruno Fernandes in the win over Arsenal, and — most importantly — was one of just four managers still holding Joao Pedro, who looks officially back and dropped 12 points.

Sometimes patience actually works. Disgusting.

⸻

🍺🚨 Bottom Two: From Penthouse to Shotgun

@⁨Zach Duguid⁩ and @⁨Tucker Cina⁩ finish in the bottom two and now owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET tomorrow morning 🍻⏰
	•	Zach’s first shotgun of the year — just one week after winning 😭
	•	Tucker’s 3rd shotgun, unfortunately very on brand

Zach captained Thiago, who blanked, and while his two transfers brought in Enzo for a +6 swing, the –4 hit wiped it out and pushed him into the danger zone. He drops out of the top 10 as fast as he entered it.

Tucker captained Haaland, got basically nothing outside of Guehi, and continues to grind through a rough season — though somehow he still sits just outside the top half.
