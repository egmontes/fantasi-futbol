Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 4 Recap: Semenyo Saves the Day

🥇 @⁨Tommy Mayer⁩ — Semenyo’s Sidekick

Tommy Mayer finally escaped irrelevance and went full Hollywood this week. He was one of only two managers to slap the armband on Semenyo, who rewarded him with a goal and an assist. Add in Gabriel’s boring-but-solid 9, Salah’s 95th-minute “oh fine I’ll save you” penalty, and Van de Ven’s monster 14 from a goal and clean sheet, and Tommy shot up to 85 points — the best single-week total of the season so far.

From the bottom feeders to the top 10 in the league standings, Tommy just proved Semenyo isn’t just a meme pick… he’s a way of life.

⸻

🥈 @⁨John King⁩ — The Royal Semenyo Gambit

John King made his first top-4 appearance of the year, and he did it by gambling hard on Semenyo, who dropped 13 points after John literally transferred him in this week. (That’s called seeing the future, folks.) He also captained Salah like half the league, got Haaland’s brace, and watched Fulham’s Andersen stroll in with an unexpected 10-pointer.

The result? A kingly second place finish, a royal wave to the haters, and a warning to the rest of the league: this man is not afraid to risk it all on Bournemouth players.

⸻

🥉 Eddy Garcia-Montes — Wildcard Warlord

After weeks of meh, Eddy finally smashed the Wildcard button and actually knew what he was doing. Salah captain? Check. Haaland brace? Check. Semenyo in the XI like a true genius? Check. His defense even remembered to show up, combining for 23 points of clean sheet joy.

This was Eddy’s first top-4 finish of the year, and it launches him straight into the top 10 of the standings. Watch out — Eddy with momentum is like Watkins with expected goals: terrifying and inevitable.

⸻

🎯 @⁨Gabriel Costa⁩ @⁨Daniel T⁩annenbaum— The Efficient Assassin

Gabriel Costa hit his first top-4 finish with a squad that basically didn’t miss. Haaland (C), Salah, Van de Ven, and plenty of scattered returns made for a death-by-a-thousand-cuts performance. No nonsense, no gimmicks, just points. He’s now up to 7th place overall, quietly lurking like the fantasy manager equivalent of a crocodile in murky water.

⸻

🍺 @⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ & @⁨‪+1 (817) 219‑7082‬⁩ — The Guinness Twins, Part II

For the second time in four weeks, Ryan and Jeff have to shotgun a Guinness at 7:30am. At this point, they might as well start a YouTube channel called “Irish Breakfast.”

Neither has made a transfer all year, which feels less like “patience” and more like “refusing to open the app.” Ryan keeps giving Watkins the captain’s armband like it’s community service, and Jeff is still putting his faith in Wood, who gave him 1 point like the clown he is.

They’ve built defenses that look like 2004 Chelsea, but their attacks look like… 2024 Chelsea. Unless they shake things up, expect more early morning beers in their future.
