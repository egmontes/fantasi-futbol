⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 9: Mbuemo Madness and a Pacifico Moment

Summary:
Gameweek 9 was pure chaos — managers flying up the table, captains going cold, and Mbuemo suddenly turning into prime Drogba. Tommy Mayer returned to glory, Lucas reminded everyone he’s the real deal, and James and Collin shared the spotlight in style. Meanwhile, a few unlucky souls (Harry, Mike Delaus, and Michael Legaz) hit rock bottom, proving once again that sometimes the Guinness gods are cruel. Oh, and yes — Tommy snagged the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week Award for trusting Mbuemo when almost no one else did.

⸻

🥇 Tommy Mayer: Redemption Season Continues

It’s official — @⁨Tommy Mayer⁩ is cooking. After years of heartbreak and bottom-half banter, Tommy has found his stride. With his second 1st-place finish of the year, he ties for most prize money earned and jumps into the top 10 in the standings.

He went all in this week, using four free transfers, and while he did ship out Van de Ven (who promptly scored 23 points), he redeemed himself by bringing in Mbuemo, who bagged two goals against Brighton. And because Tommy captained him too, he’s walking away with the coveted Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week Award — a symbol of boldness, brilliance, and a little bit of blind faith.

He was also one of just four managers to start Eze (11 points) and got another steady clean sheet from Gabriel. For a manager once known for chaos and calamity, Tommy’s 2025 arc looks more like a redemption montage.

⸻

🥈 Lucas Rodriguez: The Rookie Reigns Again

@⁨Lucas Rodriguez⁩ just won’t go away — and why should he? With his 3rd top 4 finish (tied for most this season), he regains the top spot in the league table.

He captained Haaland (who, for once, did nothing), but made up for it in style with Van de Ven’s monster 23-point performance — two goals and a clean sheet. Lucas also stayed loyal to Salah (one of only four managers to keep him), who netted in Liverpool’s loss, and his Bournemouth goalkeeper Petrovic came through with a clean sheet.

Oh, and of course — Gabriel delivered again. For a first-year manager, Lucas is playing like someone who’s been here for years. The league might need to start checking his birth certificate.

⸻

🥉 James & Collin: Shared Glory, Different Paths

@⁨James McMullen⁩ and @⁨Collin Johnston⁩ share 3rd place this week, both grabbing their second top 4 finish of the season.

James, never one to shy away from chaos, decided to captain Woltemade — a move that was bold, brave, and, well… unrewarded. But he still racked up solid points from Mbuemo and Pedro Porro, saving him from the bottom.

Collin, meanwhile, stuck with the herd and captained Haaland, but got big performances from Eze and Van de Ven, both instrumental in pulling him up the standings.

Both managers sit in the middle of the table, but this week reminded the league they’re capable of punching higher when things click.

⸻

🍺 Bottoms Up: Harry, Mike Delaus, & Michael Legaz

It’s never fun down here, but someone’s gotta do it. Unfortunately for @⁨Harry Wang⁩, @⁨Mike Delaus⁩, and @⁨Michael Legaz⁩, they’ll be the ones shotgunning a Guinness before 7:30am ET on Saturday, November 1st.

This is Mike’s second time this season and Harry and Michael’s firsts.

Harry’s week was a total blackout — no goals, no assists, no clean sheets, and a captain (Semenyo) who might as well have stayed home. Mike and Michael both captained Haaland, and both regretted it deeply. Their only solace came from an Arsenal clean sheet — which wasn’t nearly enough.

And for some added irony? Michael actually used two free transfers, which gave him just enough extra points to tie Mike… and get pulled into the bottom group anyway.
