🍒 Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 6: Triple Captain Chaos

Summary:
Gameweek 6 was all about Haaland. Triple captains were flying like confetti, late goals were breaking hearts, and somehow Bournemouth players have become the backbone of winning squads. Zach stormed back into the spotlight, Lucas took over the league lead, and Ryan + Jeff are officially on the brink of Guinness banishment.

⸻

🏆 Zach’s Late-Show Masterclass

@⁨Zach Duguid⁩ finally announces himself with his first top 4 finish of the year—and he does it in style. He slapped the Triple Captain chip on Haaland, who decided to wait until after the 90th minute to bag two goals and add an assist, for maximum chaos. His Newcastle bias actually paid off too: two Magpies, barely owned in the league, chipped in 13 points against Arsenal. Add in Bournemouth’s Semenyo and Senesi for another 14 points, and suddenly Zach is skyrocketing up the standings—16 spots to be exact—into 11th. From nowhere to knocking on the top 10 in one weekend.

⸻

🥈 Eddy Climbs Into the Top 5

Eddy is officially cooking. He also triple captained Haaland (because why not?), had Semenyo doing the usual Semenyo things, and got a massive boost from Gabriel’s last-minute winner against Newcastle. Gabriel’s 13 points were the cherry on top, pushing Eddy to his 2nd top 4 finish of the year and into the top 5 overall. Suddenly, he looks like a real contender instead of just a guy who enjoys scheduling Guinness shotguns for other people.

⸻

🥉 Lucas Grabs the League Lead

@⁨Lucas Rodriguez⁩, rookie manager and apparent fantasy prodigy, just moved into first place in the league. He triple captained Haaland like the rest of the cool kids, but also made the sharp move to transfer in Semenyo this week, pairing him with Senesi for a Cherries double. It’s only his 2nd top 4 finish of the year, but he’s making them count. The newcomer is now the man to beat, and the vets of Fantasí Fútbol are already plotting how to stop him.

⸻

🎯 Jimmy Joins the Party

It’s been a quiet start for @⁨Jimmy Romanelli⁩, the two-seasons-ago league destroyer. But this week, he’s back in the conversation. He triple captained Haaland (obviously), started Semenyo, and added Senesi for good measure. The result? His first top 4 finish of the year and a 13-spot leap into the top half of the standings at 18th. This feels like the version of Jimmy we all remember—the one who made everyone else question why they even play this game.

⸻

🍺 Rock Bottom for Mike D and Mark Wright

@⁨Mike Delaus⁩ and @⁨Mark Wright⁩ tied for dead last this week, both posting 24 points—the joint-lowest score of the season. That earns them their first Guinness shotguns of the year, due before 7:30am ET on Saturday October 4th. Their downfall? Captaining Salah for his Palace dud and putting faith in Bruno Fernandes, who somehow managed negative points thanks to a missed penalty. The only saving grace was Semenyo, but let’s be honest, everybody has him at this point. Painful week, gentlemen.
