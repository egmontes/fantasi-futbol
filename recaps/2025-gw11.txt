⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 11: Bench Boosts, Missed Pens, and Jack’s Eternal Suffering

Summary:
Gameweek 11 was all about unusual heroes: bench players, Chelsea defenders, Richarlison (??), and even Harry trying. Logan rode a bench boost to glory, Mike Delaus remembered he used to be good, John Heyer escaped the basement, and Liam and Gabe tied with wildly different vibes. Meanwhile, at the bottom… Harry extended his legendary streak of pain, and Jack continued his personal war against owning Haaland.

⸻

🥇 Logan — Bench Boost Believer

@⁨Logan Kluis⁩ bursts into the spotlight with his first top 4 finish of the year and takes 1st place — all without making a single transfer. Instead, he unleashed the mighty bench boost, and it paid off beautifully.

He captained Haaland, who missed a penalty like a washed-up Sunday leaguer but still scored a goal because he’s inevitable. Logan’s two Chelsea clean sheets and hauls from Mbuemo and Bruno Fernandes (12 combined) set up the foundation.

But the secret weapon?
Anderson’s 9 points from the bench, boosted into relevance like Logan was running a charity for forgotten Crystal Palace center backs.

He was also one of only two managers with Richarlison, who randomly remembered how to score a goal.

Logan now claws his way into the top 30, which is basically the Champions League spots compared to where he was.

⸻

🥈 Mike Delaus — Return of the King (Kind Of)

@⁨Mike Delaus⁩ records his 2nd top 4 finish and looks like the Mike of old — you know, before the part where he started tying Harry for most shotguns.

He also made no transfers, captained Haaland, and rode a monster performance from Saka, whose goal and defensive contributions earned 12 points.

Mike had Mbuemo, and was one of only four managers brave enough to start Brentford’s Thiago, who just refuses to stop scoring points and dropped a 13-piece meal.

And for dessert?
Mike was one of two managers to start Everton’s Keane, who somehow produced 15 points with a goal and clean sheet.

Mike is trending upward — pray for his future opponents.

⸻

🥉 John Heyer — Three Transfers, 22-Point Swing, Zero Regrets

@⁨John Heyer⁩ makes his grand entrance into relevance with 3rd place and his first top 4 finish of the season.

He captained Haaland (safe), started Mbuemo, Saka, and Thiago (smart), and — most importantly — made three actual tactical free transfers.

Result: +22 point swing.
Translation: This man LITERALLY transferred his way out of last place.

He jumps four spots from dead last and looks primed to keep climbing. The league’s most powerful comeback arc may be underway.

⸻

4️⃣ (tie) Liam Kelly — Chelsea Merchant

@⁨Liam Kelly⁩ logs his 2nd top 4 finish, captains Haaland, starts Saka and Mbuemo, and lets his Chelsea fandom power him via two Blues who combined for 13 points.

He’s now creeping toward the top 10, ready to remind everyone that a Chelsea fan can indeed do something right this season.

⸻

4️⃣ (tie) Gabe Costa — Bench Boost, Penalty Save, and Pain

On the other end of the spectrum, @⁨Gabriel Costa⁩ also grabs his 2nd top 4 finish, but his path was pure chaos.

He captained Mateta, who did nothing and probably owes him an apology. But Gabe used his bench boost, and that saved the day.

He’s also a Liverpool fan, which made their 3–0 loss to Man City devastating… but then Liverpool’s goalie Mamardashvili saved a penalty from Gabe’s bench (lol), giving him 7 boosted points.

Thanks to randomness, bench magic, and divine intervention, Gabe jumps into the top half of the table.

⸻

🍺 Jack & Harry — Bottom Two Bros: Pain Edition

@⁨Jack Bernatchez⁩ and @⁨Harry Wang⁩ return to their natural habitat: the bottom two.
It’s both of their 3rd such finish, and it’s Harry’s 3rd straight — a streak so impressive it should come with its own Opta graphic.

Harry even tried this week. He used his Free Hit, captained Haaland, and crafted an entire team that produced:
	•	One clean sheet
	•	Zero goals beyond Haaland 
	•	Zero assists
	•	One deeply broken spirit

Jack, meanwhile, continues his commitment to performance art:
He is the only manager in the league without Haaland, but he DOES own Doku, who smashed 11 points… on Jack’s bench.

His starters?
Only Mbuemo did anything.
His final total?
24 points — which TIES his own lowest of the year.

Consistency matters. Just not this kind.
