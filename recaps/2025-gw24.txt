Gameweek 24 Recap — Differentials, Defenders & Despair

Summary 🎢📊

This week was all about bold decisions actually working. Bruno captainers ate, Ekitike went on a heater, Sunderland somehow kept clean sheets, and a few managers found out the hard way that doing nothing is, in fact, still a decision.

⸻

🥇🧠 Mitch Maisel’s Coaching Clinic

@⁨Mitch Maisel⁩ takes 1st place for his 3rd top-4 finish and stays rock-solid in 2nd overall in the league. This was a pure process-over-results masterclass.

He made 3 free transfers for a +15 swing, bringing in and captaining Bruno Fernandes, who delivered two assists. He also transferred in Kroupi, who just refuses to stop scoring, and Ekitike, who casually dropped a brace like it was nothing.

Add in Senesi’s clean sheet (8 points) and Enzo’s late game-winning goal (another 8), and suddenly Mitch is cooking with gas. The wildest stat? 8 of his 11 starters were differentials. This wasn’t luck — this was film study 📼🔥

⸻

🥈🔴⚪ John Morcos Rises

@⁨John Morcos⁩ finishes 2nd for just his 2nd top-4 finish of the year — but what a time to do it. He rockets up 5 spots into 3rd place overall.

Like Mitch, John captained Bruno, and he stacked returns everywhere else. Cunha’s goal added 9 points, Semenyo scored again, and Gabriel delivered 8 points with a clean sheet for John’s beloved Arsenal ❤️.

But the true chaos? Two Sunderland clean sheets against Burnley for 12 points. Absolutely unhinged behavior — and wildly effective.

⸻

🥉⚖️ Aidan & Jack: Same Finish, Very Different Vibes

@⁨Aidan White⁩ and @⁨Jack Bernatchez⁩ tie for 3rd, but they are on opposite journeys.

For Aidan, this is his 5th top-4 finish (tied for league most), and he remains comfortably 1st overall. Business as usual. He transferred in and captained Bruno, had Ekitike, Enzo, and two Arsenal defenders cruising to clean sheets. Calm. Ruthless. Efficient.

For Jack, this is his 2nd top-4 finish, and a huge step in his fight to escape the basement. He went bold and captained Joao Pedro, who rewarded the faith with a goal and an assist off the bench in Chelsea’s comeback. Add two Arsenal clean sheets and Man City goals from Semenyo and Cherki, and Jack finally gets a week where the vibes didn’t collapse by Saturday morning.

⸻

🍺🚨 Bottom Two (Plus One): Alarm Clock Edition

@⁨Logan Kluis⁩, @⁨James McMullen⁩, and @⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ finish in the bottom two spots (don’t ask) and now owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on Saturday, February 7th ⏰🍻
	•	Logan is coming off last week’s dominance… straight into disaster. No transfers, Haaland only managed an assist, and Neco Williams’ red card (-2) summed it all up. Logan drops out of the top 10 and into emotional whiplash territory. He’ll be prepping for the Waste Management Open in Phoenix, which somehow feels fitting.
	•	James once again captained Aaronson — and once again got absolutely nothing for it. Even two Arsenal clean sheets and a Semenyo goal couldn’t save him. He’s taking this shotgun from California, presumably in silence.
	•	Rob captained Thiago again, bravely riding the coldest streak in the league. He’s also technically starting five Man City players (legal loophole alert 🚨), but even with all that firepower, only Semenyo showed up. Both James and Rob sit below 30th, while chaos reigns.
