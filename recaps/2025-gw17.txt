Gameweek 17 Recap — Triple Captain Chaos

Summary 🔥🎰⚽

Gameweek 17 will forever be remembered as The Week Everyone Pressed the Same Button. Triple Captain Haaland absolutely broke the game, clean sheets were flying, and the rich somehow got richer at the top. Meanwhile, one captaincy mistake and a fully dead bench sent a manager straight to last place. The margins were thin, the swings were massive, and Guinness watches are officially back on.

⸻

🥇🤝 Graham & Mitch Pattullo Share 1st — Same Playbook, Same Result 💸👑

@⁨Graham Derfner⁩ and @⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩ finish tied for first, and fittingly, they did it in almost identical fashion. Both are now comfortably top-5 managers, and Mitch also grabs the crown for most money earned on the season so far.

The backbone of both teams?
	•	Triple Captain Haaland, who casually dropped two goals and an assist against West Ham
	•	Semenyo, who simply refuses to stop scoring
	•	A healthy collection of defensive clean sheets

Where they differed is what made this fun. Graham leaned on Ekitike, who scored yet again and continues his “why didn’t I own him sooner” campaign. Mitch countered with Rogers, who somehow scored two goals again, because apparently that’s just his thing now. Two managers, two slightly different paths, one shared throne.

⸻

🥉 Eric Takes 3rd — Goalkeeper Merchants Win Again 🧤🚀

@⁨Eric Wallerstein⁩ lands in 3rd place for his third top-4 finish of the year, pushing himself firmly into the top 5 of the standings.

Yes, he also triple captained Haaland. Yes, he also had Rogers. But the real flex was between the posts. Kelleher delivered a monster performance against Wolves with a clean sheet and a penalty save, good for 13 points and a reminder that goalkeepers can, in fact, win weeks.

Add in two Man City clean sheets at the back and Eric quietly put together one of the most well-rounded teams of the week.

⸻

🏅 Logan Finishes 4th — Consistency King 📈🔵

@⁨Logan Kluis⁩ grabs 4th place, earning his fourth top-4 finish of the year — tied for the most in the league — and officially enters the top half of the table.

He kept things simple:
	•	Haaland captained
	•	Semenyo and Rogers both delivering again
	•	Two Man City defenders locking down clean sheets

The cherry on top was Verbruggen, who racked up 11 points thanks to a clean sheet and six saves. Logan didn’t do anything flashy — he just did everything right, which is becoming a theme.

⸻

🍺 Liam Kelly & John Heyer Bottom Out — One Captain Choice Away 😬🥴

@⁨Liam Kelly⁩ and @⁨John Heyer⁩ finish in the bottom two and owe a Guinness shotgun.

John’s week completely unraveled due to one brutal decision: captaining Saka instead of Haaland. To make matters worse, he started Mbuemo while he was at AFCON, and somehow his entire bench didn’t play either, dropping him all the way to 37th place. A nightmare scenario.

Liam did at least captain Haaland, but his midfield completely disappeared in a week where goals and assists were everywhere. The result nudges him out of the top half and into uncomfortable territory.

⸻

Another week where Haaland ruled, small decisions had massive consequences, and the league table continues to tighten at the top. On to Gameweek 18 — and maybe next time, check if your bench is alive. 😅
