🎆⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 28 Recap ⚽🎆

📰 Summary — Wildcards, Bench Boosts & Haaland Ghosting

Gameweek 28 was pure chaos. Haaland didn’t play, offenses exploded anyway, and two managers weaponized their chips like seasoned FPL assassins. Wildcards hit. Bench boosts paid. Goalkeepers lucked into clean sheets. Meanwhile, two unfortunate souls are preparing for an early-morning Guinness. Let’s unpack it.

⸻

🥇🤝 Jimmy & Rob — Double Trouble at the Top

🔥 @⁨Jimmy Romanelli⁩ — The Wildcard Wizard

Jimmy grabs a share of 1st place, marking his 4th top-four finish, and vaults himself into the top 10 overall. The catalyst? A perfectly timed Wildcard.

He boldly captained Ekitike, who ended his drought in style with 1 goal and 2 assists. In midfield, Bruno Fernandes and Harry Wilson each delivered a goal and an assist. At the back, O’Reilly held a clean sheet, Virgil scored, and Gabriel added an assist for good measure.

And the best part? Jimmy didn’t even need Haaland this week. Zero minutes. Zero panic. The Wildcard was a masterpiece.

⸻

💪 @⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ — Bench Boost Bandit

Rob matches Jimmy at the top for his 5th top-four finish of the season… and somehow still sits comfortably in the bottom half of the table. Consistency? Yes. Table position? Confusing.

Rob made the bold call to captain Thiago, who delivered with a goal. He also went Haaland-less, which aged beautifully. Like Jimmy, he benefited from O’Reilly and Gabriel, but Rob added Saliba, who scored for Arsenal, and Bruno Fernandes in midfield.

The real difference-maker? Bench Boost. Rob piled on 28 points from his bench, led by Harry Wilson and Ekitike. That chip did serious damage.

⸻

🥉🤝 Mitch Pattullo & Tucker — Ekitike Enthusiasts

⚡ @⁨Mitch Pattullo⁩

Mitch secures his 7th top-four finish (tied for league most) and continues holding strong in 2nd overall. He captained Ekitike, doubled down with Gabriel, and had Timber, who scored Arsenal’s second goal. He also rolled with Semenyo, who somehow scored City’s lone goal.

Mitch’s biggest break? Kelleher didn’t play, allowing backup José Sá to step in with 8 points thanks to Wolves’ shock clean sheet against Aston Villa. Sometimes luck is a strategy.

⸻

🚀 @⁨Tucker Cina⁩

For Tucker, this is only his 2nd top-four finish, but it launches him into the top half of the table. He also captained Ekitike, had Gabriel, and smartly transferred in O’Reilly, which proved to be a huge move.

Tucker paired Semenyo with Bruno Fernandes, forming a tidy little midfield combo that delivered when it mattered.

⸻

🍺💀 Bottom Two — Captain Chaos

😬 @⁨Luke Civantos⁩ — The Zero Goal Experience

Luke lands in the bottom two for the first time this season and now owes a Guinness shotgun. He captained Gabriel, which was respectable… but the rest of his team forgot to function.

He had four players not play at all, which forced him into starting Haaland for 0 points. In a week filled with attacking fireworks, Luke’s squad did not score a single goal. Not one. He now drops out of the top half.

⸻

🫠 @⁨John Heyer⁩ — Double Points? Never Heard of Them

John earns his second Guinness shotgun of the season. He captained Haaland, vice-captained Foden, and neither played. Zero double points. That’s tough to overcome.

His team did manage to score a couple of goals, but without any captain multiplier, it wasn’t enough. John is also sitting on five free transfers that he refuses to use. Hoarding season continues.
