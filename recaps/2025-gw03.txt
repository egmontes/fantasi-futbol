⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 3 Recap

“The Kelly Brothers Take Over” Edition

⸻

🥇 @⁨Liam Kelly⁩ — First Place, 81 Points, Midfield Masterclass

Liam Kelly dropped a masterpiece this week, setting a new season-high with 81 points and reminding everyone that sometimes… you just have to trust Jack Grealish’s calves. Liam was the only manager brave enough to start both Senesi (14 points from another chaotic Bournemouth outing) and Grealish (10 points from his first appearance in Liam’s squad). Add in Dewsbury-Hall, who somehow decided Everton was worth a goal + assist performance (12 points), and Bruno Fernandes with 10 — Liam’s midfield was basically an all-you-can-eat buffet of returns.

To top it off, Liam was one of just two managers to captain Joao Pedro, who rewarded him with a goal. The result? Not only the Gameweek win, but Liam now sits top of the entire league. From high score of the season to league leader in one fell swoop — not bad work for one weekend.

⸻

🥈 @⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ — Second Place, Keeping It in the Family

The Kelly brothers were clearly cooking this week. Ryan Kelly finished just behind his younger brother, making it a 1–2 finish for Clan Kelly. Ryan showed why older siblings often know best: he was one of only 5 managers to start Guehi, who delivered a clean sheet and a goal for 15 points. His midfield pairing of Bruno Fernandes and Mbuemo combined for 22 points, and up top Bowen and Beto chipped in another 16. It was a balanced, steady performance that kept Ryan safe at the top end of the standings. The Kelly household is officially running this league right now.

⸻

🥉 @⁨Eric Wallerstein⁩ & @⁨Lucas Rodriguez⁩ — Tied in Third

It’s only Gameweek 3 and already the newbies are making noise. Lucas Rodriguez, in his debut season, grabbed his first top-4 finish — and he did it by rolling out Haaland (captain, goal) and Joao Pedro, plus a clever transfer spree to bring in both Haaland and Dewsbury-Hall (yep, him again). Eric, on the other hand, leaned on Guehi’s 15-point haul to complement Haaland’s captaincy return.

Both end up tied for 3rd, and more importantly, both are now sitting inside the league’s top 5 overall. For Lucas, it’s a statement: he’s not here just to fill out the league. For Eric, it’s a reminder that sometimes patience pays off. Either way, they’re climbing fast.

⸻

🍺 @⁨Jack Bernatchez⁩ & @⁨Robert Shellow⁩ — The Guinness Crew

Ouch. Someone’s got to pay the price, and this week it’s Jack Bernatchez and newcomer Robert Shellow. Both managers captained strikers who completely blanked, leaving their scorelines embarrassingly bare. Jack scraped a few points from defensive contributions, while Robert’s only real highlight was Munoz’s clean sheet. That’s it. That’s the list.

The real sting for Jack? This rough week drops him all the way into last place in the league. Now both he and Robert will be forced to shotgun a Guinness before 7:30am ET on either Saturday September 6th or 13th — giving them two dates to practice their technique. Welcome to the league, Robert. For Jack… we’ll just call this “rock bottom.”
