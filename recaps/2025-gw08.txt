⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 8: Arsenal Fans Rise, Legends Fall

Summary:
Gameweek 8 was a week for redemption, revelation, and record-breaking ridiculousness. Arsenal fans flexed, Palace players partied, and Haaland kept ruining everyone’s differential dreams. John Morcos exploded for a league-record 96 points, Robert Shellow continued his early-season domination, and Mateta suddenly became the most popular man in Fantasí Fútbol. Meanwhile, former greats Mark Wright and Dave Bambrick have plummeted to the depths of despair — and the bottom two — where only Guinness shotguns await.

⸻

🥇 @⁨John Morcos⁩: From Midtable to Marvelous

96 points. The new all-time single-week record. John Morcos, Arsenal diehard and eternal optimist, finally saw his patience rewarded after hitting the Wildcard button this week — and it worked like magic. He captained Haaland (2 goals, obviously), fielded five defenders and a goalkeeper who combined for 43 points, and was one of six managers with hat-trick hero Mateta. Two Arsenal defenders plus Raya gave him a 24-point foundation, and from there, everything just clicked. John vaults into the top half of the table (11th place) and officially becomes the first manager in league history to score high enough to make other people’s screens fog up from jealousy.

⸻

🥈 @⁨Robert Shellow⁩: The Relentless Rookie

Another week, another podium. First-year manager Robert Shellow just keeps delivering. With his third top 4 finish of the season, Robert sits first overall, has the most prize money, and looks like the manager to beat. He doubled down on his Arsenal loyalty by transferring in Timber, joining his fellow defenders and Raya for 24 clean-sheet points. He captained Haaland, owned Mateta, and even got a cheeky 10 points from Munoz. To top it off, he was one of only two managers with Rogers, who scored for Aston Villa. The man can’t stop winning — and he hasn’t even used a chip yet.

⸻

🥉 @⁨Michael Legaz⁩: The Lucky Legend

Michael Legaz finally cracked the top 4 for the first time this season — and let’s be honest, luck was on his side. Despite having five free transfers available, he decided to do absolutely nothing and started three injured players who didn’t play a single minute. But his bench bailed him out: Mateta and Esteve both came in swinging, with Esteve keeping a clean sheet for Burnley. Add in 29 points from his other defenders and goalkeeper, and somehow, Michael stumbled his way to glory. Sometimes it’s better to be lucky than good — and Michael just proved it.

⸻

🎯 @⁨Graham Derfner⁩: Back in Business

After a slow start to the season, Graham Derfner has officially arrived. His first-ever top 4 finish of the year rockets him to 4th place overall in the standings. He captained Haaland, brought in Gabriel (who rewarded him with 12 points), and had the red-hot Crystal Palace duo of Mateta and Munoz. The only blemish? He transferred out Rogers, who finally scored for the first time all season — a decision that might haunt him for at least 48 hours. Still, a stellar week for Graham, who’s proving he belongs near the top.

⸻

🍺 The Guinness Gauntlet: @⁨Mark Wright⁩ & @⁨Dave Bambrick⁩ in Despair

Oh, how the mighty have fallen. Mark Wright and Dave Bambrick — both top managers last year — are now sitting in 36th and 35th place respectively after each landed in the bottom two for the second time this season. Neither owns Haaland, which should honestly be a crime at this point.

Mark made no transfers, captained Salah (who continues to underwhelm), and got modest points only from Bruno Fernandes and Sanchez. Dave, meanwhile, made four transfers (and took a -4 hit), captained Ekitike (who didn’t even start for Liverpool), and watched his team collapse faster than his pint of Guinness.

Dave’s shotgun is due before 7:30am Japan time this Saturday while he’s traveling — truly poetic justice. Mark’s is due Saturday, October 25th before 7:30am ET. Consider this your official public reminder, lads.
