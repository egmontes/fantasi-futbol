Gameweek 18 Recap — The Week Haaland Took a Nap

Summary 🌀🍺🎯

Gameweek 18 gave us everything: galaxy-brain captaincy calls, a hat trick no one saw coming, bench boosts doing actual work, and the rare sighting of a Haaland triple captain doing… absolutely nothing. The top of the table shuffled again, the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week award found a very deserving home, and the Guinness list unfortunately got longer.

⸻

🥇🎰 Mark Takes 1st — Pure, Unfiltered Chaos Ball

@⁨Mark Wright⁩ grabs 1st place for his league-leading 5th top-4 finish, jumping nine spots straight into the top 10. This week was a masterclass in reckless confidence paying off.

Mark earns the Pacifico™️ Impulse Captain of the Week award by captaining Wirtz, who he literally transferred in this week and who promptly scored his first Premier League goal of the season. Incredible scenes.

As if that wasn’t enough, Mark was also the only manager brave (or unwell) enough to start Schade, who casually dropped a hat trick for Brentford. While the rest of the league was playing checkers, Mark was flipping the board over and still winning.

⸻

🥈🪑 Zach Finishes 2nd — Bench Boost Merchants Stay Winning

@⁨Zach Duguid⁩ rolls into 2nd place for his third top-4 finish of the year and officially enters the top half of the table.

His starting XI did just enough — two Arsenal midfielders combining for 12 points and Semenyo continuing his weekly scoring habit — but the real damage came from the bench. Zach’s bench boost added 26 points, led by Dubravka’s 11 and Dominic Calvert-Lewin’s 8.

When the starters stall, sometimes you just let the bench cook.

⸻

🥉📊 Mitch Maisel Gets 3rd — Quietly Still Elite

@⁨Mitch Maisel⁩ finishes 3rd for only his second top-4 finish of the season… and yet somehow still sits 2nd overall in the league. Efficiency.

Like Zach, Mitch benefited from Dubravka and Calvert-Lewin, but he also found value where almost no one else did. He was one of only two managers to start Cherki, who delivered a goal and an assist, and the only manager brave enough to roll out Tarkowski, whose 8 points came courtesy of Everton’s clean sheet.

Not flashy, not loud — just relentlessly effective.

⸻

🏅📈 Tommy Harris Gets 4th — Liverpool Loyalty Pays Off

@⁨Tommy Harris⁩ lands 4th place and officially enters the top 10 of the league table.

He leaned on the usual suspects — Semenyo doing Semenyo things, Van de Ven’s 11 points from Spurs’ clean sheet, and Saka’s steady 7 — but the difference came from backing his heart.

Tommy trusted Liverpool, getting an assist from Ekitike and a goal from Gravenberch, making him one of just three managers to start Gravenberch this week. Emotional investing worked out for once.

⸻

🍺🚨 Robert, Liam Kelly & Tucker — When Haaland Betrays You

@⁨Robert Shellow⁩, @⁨Liam Kelly⁩, and @⁨Tucker Cina⁩ finish in the bottom two spots and owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on Saturday, January 3rd.

All three captained Haaland, and Robert even triple captained him — unfortunately choosing the one week he decided to do absolutely nothing. Across all three teams, there was only one goal and one clean sheet total, and far too many players dropping one-pointers or worse.

Sometimes the chalk fails. This was one of those times.

No excuses. No extensions. Alarm clocks are mandatory.
