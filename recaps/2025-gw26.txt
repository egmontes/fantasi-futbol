🎭⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 26 Recap ⚽🎭

📰 Summary — Double Gameweek Drama

Gameweek 26 gave us everything: redemption arcs, triple captain chaos, defenders scoring like strikers, and two managers who now need to emotionally prepare for a sunrise Guinness. Arsenal’s double gameweek created massive swings, Rice became a cult hero, and somehow the bottom half continues to move like a lava lamp. Let’s break it down.

⸻

🥇👑 Tyler — From Shotgun to Champion

@⁨Tyler Wasser⁩ completes the ultimate bounce-back, winning 1st place for his 2nd top-four finish of the season just one week after finishing dead last. No transfers. No panic. Just vibes.

He captained Gabriel, which turned out to be pretty average given Arsenal failed to keep a clean sheet in either match of their double. But the rest of his squad showed up in a big way. He was one of only three managers to start Virgil, who casually dropped 17 points with a goal and clean sheet. Even crazier, Tyler was the only manager to start Hincapié, who somehow found 15 points during Arsenal’s double gameweek madness.

Tyler now creeps toward the top half of the table like a man reborn.

⸻

🥈🚀 Rob — The Five-Transfer Frenzy

@⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ grabs 2nd place for his 4th top-four finish and vaults seven spots up the table, officially escaping the 30s. Freedom.

He was one of just three managers to start Thiaw and one of three to start O’Reilly, both delivering double-digit returns from defense. But the headline? Rob triple captained Declan Rice during Arsenal’s double gameweek — and it actually worked. Rice piled up defensive contributions and an assist, making the bold differential play pay off.

Now, Rob did burn five free transfers, which somehow resulted in a net loss of 5 points… but when you finish 2nd, who’s counting?

⸻

🥉🔥 Collin — Mr. Consistency

@⁨Collin Johnston⁩ secures 3rd place, marking his 6th top-four finish, tied for league most. The man refuses to go away. He’s now knocking on the door of the top 10.

Like Rob, Collin triple captained Rice, riding the Arsenal double to glory. He also made four free transfers for a net gain of 5 points, which sounds modest… until you realize that without those five points, he would’ve fallen out of the top four entirely. Margins. Precision. Surgical execution.

⸻

🏅🤝 The 4-Way Tie for 4th — Same Score, Different Stories

This week’s chaos produced a four-way tie for 4th between @⁨John Morcos⁩, @⁨JCarr Gamble⁩, @⁨Mike Delaus⁩, and @⁨Liam Kelly⁩, and somehow it reshuffled the entire table.
	•	John Morcos triple captained Gabriel for a respectable-but-not-explosive return and made three free transfers for a +4 gain, which was enough to move him all the way up into 2nd overall. Efficient climbing.
	•	Mike also triple captained Gabriel, but his key move was bringing in Rice for a +12 net gain. Despite matching the others’ weekly score, he somehow slides further within the bottom half. The table makes no sense.
	•	JCarr kept it simple and captained the red-hot João Pedro, who delivered 10 points and kept his heater alive. No drama, just returns.
	•	Liam Kelly went with a regular captain on Gabriel and quietly made three free transfers for a massive +16 net gain. And yet… he still drops within the bottom half. Fantasy is cruel.

⸻

🍺💀 Bottom Two — Bruno Betrayal

It’s a painful week for @⁨Liam Cashman⁩ and @⁨Mitch Maisel⁩, who finish bottom two and now owe a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on February 21st.

Both captained Bruno Fernandes, who finally cooled off after weeks of carrying managers on his back.

For Liam, disaster struck when he left Rice on the bench during his productive double gameweek. A season-defining error.

For Mitch, even a respectable +5 net gain from transfers couldn’t overcome the fact that his squad simply forgot how to score. He drops out of the top three, while Liam tumbles out of the top half. Brutal.
