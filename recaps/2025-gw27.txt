🎪⚽ Fantasí Fútbol – Gameweek 27 Recap ⚽🎪

📰 Summary — The 56-Point Masterpiece

Gameweek 27 will be remembered for one thing: winning with 56 points. Clean sheets were rare, goals were limited, and assists were apparently optional. It was a week where mediocrity triumphed, chaos reigned, and three managers forgot that attacking returns are generally helpful in fantasy football.

⸻

🥇💰 Robert — Winning Ugly, Winning Often

@⁨Robert Shellow⁩ claims 1st place for his league-leading 7th top-four finish and his 3rd win of the season, extending his lead as the manager who has earned the most money this year.

And yet… somehow… he still sits outside the top 10 in the overall table. Make it make sense.

Even better? This victory ties his own record for the lowest winning score ever — 56 points. Historic stuff. Robert made zero transfers, captained Haaland (who managed an assist), and watched the rest of his team slowly piece together a win through pure vibes.

João Pedro stayed hot with another goal, while Mbeumo, Harry Wilson, Timber, and Konsa all chipped in with assists. No clean sheet defenders. Just one actual goal scored across the squad. Truly fascinating to win a week like this. It wasn’t dominance — it was survival.

⸻

🥈🤝 The Five-Way Tie for 2nd — Chaos Collective

Eddy, @⁨Graham Derfner⁩, @⁨James McMullen⁩, @⁨Andrew Denucci⁩, and @⁨Ryan Kelly⁩ all tie for 2nd in a week where apparently nobody wanted to fully separate from the pack.
	•	Eddy and Graham ride this result into the top 5 overall, continuing their steady climb.
	•	James and Andrew use the boost to finally sneak out from below 30th.
	•	Ryan inches closer to freedom from the 30s, but he’s not fully safe yet.

The paths to success were split down the middle. Eddy, James, and Andrew captained the red-hot João Pedro, who continued his heater. Meanwhile, Ryan and Graham benefited from starting O’Reilly, who casually scored two goals as a defender for Man City because of course he did.

Different strategies. Same result. Maximum confusion.

⸻

🍺💀 Bottom Spot — The Assist Allergy Trio

It’s a three-way tie at the bottom for @⁨Zach Duguid⁩, @⁨Collin Johnston⁩, and @⁨Richard Katz⁩, and that means a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET this Saturday.
	•	It’s Collin’s first of the season.
	•	Zach’s second.
	•	Katz’s third. A veteran at this point.

All three managers had Haaland, which should’ve helped. It didn’t. None of them recorded a single defensive clean sheet. In fact, they took things a step further by producing almost no assists whatsoever. It was a quiet week league-wide — but these three turned quiet into complete silence.

Katz made the only transfer among them, and it rewarded him with a –5 point swing, which is almost poetic.

Zach and Collin drift back toward mid-table mediocrity, while Katz remains parked below 30th, contemplating life choices.
