⚽ Fantasí Fútbol Gameweek 10: The CDM Revolution

Summary:
It’s official — defensive midfielders are taking over Fantasí Fútbol. Lucas, the rookie phenom, blew everyone out of the water with a 99-point masterclass powered by Caicedo, Rice, and Xhaka. Haaland kept doing Haaland things, and Arsenal defenders once again ruled the day. Collin and Mark rediscovered their form, Rob DiSanto finally found some, and the shotgun committee got busy again — with Andrew, Harry, and Mike Delaus all due for a frothy 7:30am wake-up call.

⸻

🥇 Lucas Rodriguez: The Rookie Becomes the King

@⁨Lucas Rodriguez⁩ is unstoppable. With his 4th top 4 finish — the most in the league — and his first 1st-place finish, he’s now sitting firmly atop the standings with the most prize money earned and a league-high 99 points, the top weekly score of the season.

He captained Haaland, who bagged two goals again, but that was just the beginning. Lucas somehow assembled a midfield of Caicedo, Rice, and Xhaka, and all three of them scored 10+ points. That’s not a midfield — that’s a brick wall that learned how to shoot.

Gabriel added 12 more points with a clean sheet and goal, Mateta scored again, and Salah (one of only three managers who still believed in him) chipped in too.

But the move of the week? Transferring in Xhaka, a decision that swung his score by 9 points over Reijnders. He’s making this whole fantasy thing look easy.

⸻

🥈 Collin: The Arsenal Whisperer

@⁨Collin Johnston⁩’s back — and this time, he’s looking dangerous. With his 3rd top 4 finish of the season, he’s climbing quickly toward the top half of the table. After a rough debut campaign last year, this is a major glow-up.

He, too, captained Haaland, and leaned into his usual Arsenal core: Gabriel and Rice both delivered again — but the real twist was Xhaka, now somehow lighting it up at Sunderland, chipping in another double-digit score. Throw in a solid 10-point showing from Guehi, and Collin’s week looked like something straight out of Mikel Arteta’s tactical dreams… if Sunderland were suddenly good.

If he keeps this up, Collin might finally have something to text in the group chat other than “pain.”

⸻

🥉 Mark Wright: Back from the Bottom

Well, well, well — @⁨Mark Wright⁩ is back. After being a shotgun casualty last week, Mark decided enough was enough. He delivered his first top 4 finish of the year, channeling the form that made him a breakout star in his debut season.

Despite making no transfers (again), Mark put together a quietly lethal squad. He captained Haaland, stacked three Arsenal defenders (including Gabriel), and got surprise goals from Ndiaye and Minteh — both rare picks who scored 9 apiece. Joao Pedro’s goal added another 9 to his total, rounding out a redemption week worthy of applause.

He’s still below 30th, but this is the kind of week that starts a comeback arc.

⸻

🏆 Rob DiSanto: Clean Sheet Comeback

@⁨Rob DiSanto⁩ finally cracks the top 4 with his first top 4 finish of the year — and honestly, it’s about time. A fourth-year veteran sitting in 34th place needed this.

He captained Haaland, leaned on Rice and Salah for attacking points, and got clean sheets across his defense without even having Gabriel. Add in Joao Pedro’s goal and Rob’s team quietly stacked up the points like a guy who finally read the rulebook.

A long way to go, but the foundation is there — and the taste of a Guinness-free Saturday morning has to feel good.

⸻

🍺 Bottoms Up: Andrew, Harry & Mike Delaus

It’s a crowded penalty box this week — @⁨Andrew Denucci⁩, @⁨Harry Wang⁩, and @⁨Mike Delaus⁩ all find themselves owing a Guinness shotgun before 7:30am ET on Saturday, November 8th.

It’s Andrew’s first ever, Harry’s second, and Mike’s third, tying him for the league lead. To make matters worse, it’s back-to-back weeks for Harry and Mike.

All three are seasoned managers, but their seasons have been rougher than Cucurella’s defending.
	•	Andrew captained Haaland but had zero players (other than him) score more than 8 points.
	•	Mike captained Haaland too, but his four transfers netted -12 points total compared to the players he sold — a brutal swing that tanked his week even further.
	•	Harry captained Mbuemo, who responded by doing absolutely nothing. His two transfers netted him -7 points, making it a painful week all around.

They actually had the highest scores ever for a shotgun week, which somehow makes it even funnier.
